Grading Policy

This course is graded based on paper presentations, a midterm exam, and an LLM-enhanced midterm report.

Components

Component Weight
Paper Presentations & Discussions75%
Midterm Exam5%
LLM-Enhanced Midterm Report20%

Paper Presentations & Discussions (75%)

  • Each student presents 2-3 times during the quarter.
  • Presentations are based on curated research papers organized by topic.
  • Presenters must lead class discussion covering:
    • Paper strengths and contributions
    • Weaknesses and limitations
    • Future research directions

Midterm Exam (5%)

  • Date: November 6, 2025 (during class time)
  • Format: Closed book, online
  • Duration: Distributed at the beginning of class; submit on Canvas within 1 hour 20 minutes
  • Scope: Fundamental quantum computing system concepts covered in lectures
  • Grading: Graded mostly for participation; graded together with the LLM-enhanced report
  • Honesty policy: Work independently -- no collaboration allowed during the exam

LLM-Enhanced Midterm Report (20%)

  • Due: December 8, 2025 by 11:59pm
  • Task: Re-do the midterm exam using LLM tools (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, etc.)
  • Report must include:
    • How AI tools were used to answer each question
    • How AI-generated answers were verified and/or corrected
    • Reflections on AI strengths and weaknesses in quantum computing
  • Bonus: Novel results discovered through this process may become co-authored research papers.

Presentation Topics

Student presentations are based on curated research papers organized by the following topics. Each student presents 2-3 times and must lead a class discussion analyzing strengths, weaknesses, and future directions of the presented work.

Gate Synthesis & General Compiler Optimizations

Research on quantum gate decomposition, synthesis algorithms, and general-purpose compiler optimization passes for quantum circuits.

Superconducting Architectures

Systems and compiler challenges specific to superconducting qubit platforms, including connectivity constraints and crosstalk mitigation.

Ion Trap Systems

Compilation and optimization for trapped-ion quantum computers, including all-to-all connectivity and shuttling operations.

Neutral Atom Systems

Emerging neutral atom quantum computing platforms, including reconfigurable atom arrays and native multi-qubit gates.

Photonics Quantum Computing

Linear optical quantum computing, boson sampling, and photonic integrated circuits for quantum information processing.

QEC: Syndrome Circuit Compiling

Compilation techniques for quantum error correction syndrome extraction circuits, including ancilla management and scheduling.

QEC: Logic-Level Compiling

Compilation at the logical qubit level, including lattice surgery, code deformation, and logical gate scheduling.

QEC: Decoding Optimizations

Efficient decoding algorithms for quantum error correction, including MWPM, union-find, and neural network decoders.

Advanced QEC Topics

Cutting-edge research in quantum error correction including new code families, fault-tolerant protocols, and hardware-aware QEC.
